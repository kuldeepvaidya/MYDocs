Wholesale Banking examines the strategic role of technology in the global delivery of banking services to commercial, corporate, and institutional customers. It covers not only the major lines of wholesale business - commercial lending, corporate finance, cash management, noncredit banking services, trade services, and corporate trust - but also the compelling cross-business issues now facing wholesale bank executives, such as risk management, operational efficiency, customer relationship management (CRM), and outsourcing.


Wholesale Banking services between merchant banks and other financial institutions. In other words, it's also called as business to business banking. 

Wholesale Banking can be classified in to the following business areas with indicative functional areas: 
Commercial Lending  
Cash Management 
Trade Finance  
Treasury Services  
Structured Finance 
Financial Messaging
